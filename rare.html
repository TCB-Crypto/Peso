Undervalued & Rare Philippine Coins Often Mislisted on eBay

1. 1971 Marcos 1 Piso Proof Coin (KM# 211)

  • Real value: $10–$25 in proof condition
  • Common listing title: “Philippines 1 Peso 1971 coin”
  • Tip: Look for mirror-like proof surfaces. Many sellers don’t know it's a proof coin.

2. 1964 MacArthur Commemorative Peso (Silver)

  • Real value: $15–$40 depending on condition
  • Often mislisted as: “commemorative token”
  • Tip: Search “1964 peso” and avoid listings with poor descriptions.

3. 1947-S Reverse Mint Mark Peso

  • Real value: $20–$80+
  • Mislisted as: Regular 1947 Peso
  • Tip: Check mintmark placement. The "S" should be on the reverse, not obverse.

4. U.S.-Philippines Silver Coins (1903–1912)

  • Real value: $10–$150+ depending on coin & condition
  • Often mislisted as: Just “old coin” with no silver mention
  • Tip: Watch for dates 1903–1912 and denominations like 1 Peso, 50 Centavos.

5. Error Coins & Off-Center Modern Coins

  • Real value: $10–$200+ for dramatic errors
  • Commonly sold for: 1–2 pesos or face value
  • Tip: Look for off-center strikes, double dies, or rotated coins.

6. English Series Banknotes (1951+)

  • Real value: $15–$100+ depending on denomination
  • Mislisted as: “old paper money” with no mention of “English Series”
  • Tip: Notes are fully in English and predate the Filipino language series.

7. WWII Japanese Invasion Notes

  • Commonly sold: in cheap lots
  • Valuable types: Error notes, uncirculated, or full sheets
  • Tip: Look for serial anomalies, overprints, or pristine notes.
